Engine Documentation

A governed, human-in-the-loop engine that investigates an incident and produces an evidence-backed root cause. It is pluggable exactly where pluggability pays — the reasoning model sits behind one Planner contract, every tool is a small adapter behind one transport seam, and the problem domain (phases, gates, tunables, prompt doctrine) is a playbook file — and deliberately concrete everywhere else: one assertion store, one mutation seam, one append-only journal of record, and one simple file-backed data layer (runtime/store.py — a contained module, not a pluggable storage backend).

§1What this is

The engine runs an incident investigation as a governed loop. A planner — a live LLM or a deterministic script, interchangeable behind the same contract (runtime/planner.py) — proposes typed operations; the reducer (graph/reducer.py materialize) validates them against a closed vocabulary, quarantining what it cannot name rather than erasing it; one fold (graph/fold.py apply_delta) — the single mutation seam — applies the surviving delta to the two projections the planner reasons over, the Graph of the system under investigation and the Hypothesis store of competing explanations; a declarative gate (runtime/controller.py check_gate) decides whether the phase may advance; and the full delta is then appended to the third projection, the Journal of record (Journal.append_phase — deliberately a separate call from the fold, so an interactive write-gate can hold a computed-but-unapplied delta pending human approval). Because every phase entry carries its full delta, rebuild(journal) replays those entries through the same fold and reproduces graph + hypothesis store bit-for-bit. Belief is earned, not asserted: evidence weighs by source reliability × temporal proximity × topological specificity (hypothesis/belief.py), and a hypothesis is promoted only when its engine-earned score crosses the gate with no unrefuted rival alive. Anything that would write to a live system stops at a human approval gate first.

The packaged playbook (playbooks/incident.yaml) declares the five phases every run moves through: frame → investigate (ONE loop — hypothesize ⇄ gather evidence ⇄ refute, gated on promotion and an attempted refutation) → act (the sole writes_allowed phase — every production-changing action, early mitigation and remediation alike, is the same human-gated machinery, not a phase of its own) → verify (recovery is proven by an active cleared-event on the symptom node, never asserted; a failed recovery backtracks to investigate) → close (the postmortem, folded from the journal). Triage and remediate are retired as phases (owner-ratified): triage's one real decision — mitigate now vs investigate — is the act-entry decision, available from investigate at any time; remediate's real success — an approved, executed, journaled write — is an action inside act, not a stage.

Four ground rules the whole system is built around: the journal is the source of truth (every phase entry carries its full delta; replay reproduces the state); belief is earned (promotion is the engine's decision — the model's self-reported confidence is only a prior); writes are human-gated (a write-effect capability executes only under an approved gate); and nothing observed is silently erased (unknowns land quarantined and provisional; every rejection is journaled and fed back to the next plan).

Who reads what — the projection roles. The three projections split cleanly by consumer. The journal is the audit/review record and the replay/resume substrate — it is never a planner reasoning input. What the planner sees each turn is the PlanContext the engine builds from the other two projections: the graph's bounded focus slice (graph/tools.py focus_slice — cause path + suspects + frontier in full, healthy collapsed to counts), the ranked hypothesis summary (id, statement, status, engine-earned score, root candidate, supporting/refuting evidence counts), and the engine-computed skew-tolerant change→onset correlations (belief.correlate_timeline) — plus the last gate's failure reason and the last step's reducer rejections, carried on engine state. Review and postmortems read the journal; reasoning reads the graph and the hypothesis store.
